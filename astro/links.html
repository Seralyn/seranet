Getting Started with Astronomy

Software

There are many types of software that can greatly aid the efficacy and enjoyment of this hobby for the modern-day hobbyist astronomer. First and foremost among them is probably planetarium software. These programs allow you to get a complete look at the night sky, according to your location, time-zone, and lattitude. They allow you to locate and track objects of interest in order, whether your goal is to simply observe them or to photograph them. They also facilitate the planning of viewing or photography sessions in advance so as to plan ahead for dark sky site visits or setting up for a night of imaging.

>

Aside from planetarium software and whether you are using a dedicated astrocam or DSLR/mirrorless camera, capture software can greatly aid you in capturing the raw images you will need to get great images. After the capture phase, the best images you can produce will typically involve a process known as "stacking". In short, stacking combines each of your slightly flawed frames to produce one beautiful master frame, which will then be edited in other programs.

After the stacking process, arguably the longest and most arduous aspect of astrophotography begins. Editing. There are a multitude of programs that this process can be undertaken in, many of which aid the capture and stacking process under one roof. Almost all of these programs have aspects that they excel in and others in which they do not. There is no one "best" program. Most of them have free trials but I recommend experimenting with the free trials offered by most of them to find out which is right for you.
